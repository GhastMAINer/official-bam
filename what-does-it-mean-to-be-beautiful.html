      "What Does It Mean To Be Beautiful?"

      February 2, 2025

      Introduction

      “There Was This Really Fat Girl”, written by Carla M. Pacis, stands out as a powerful literary work that addresses the themes of body image, identity, and societal expectations. Through the voice of a young female protagonist, the narrative confronts her struggles while dealing with the harsh judgments and stereotypes imposed by society regarding her size. The story effectively captures her internal battles, illustrating how her self-perception is influenced by external pressures as well as her personal experiences. With a striking blend of humor and distress, this piece encourages readers to reflect on the universal quest for acceptance, love, and authenticity in a world that often prioritizes physical appearance over individuality. It challenges the stigma surrounding body weight and emphasizes the critical importance of self-acceptance and resilience in overcoming adversity


      Summary

      "There Was This Really Fat Girl" explored the negative effects of society pressures on body image, concentrating on the emotional and social effects that a young girl has as a result of her weight. The main character of the story is a young girl in her teenage years, who struggles with her weight and the demands society has on her beauty. It occurred in a bathroom of the house, mall, school, clinic, gym, and prom where the girl experiences discrimination and prejudice. It is situated in the present and reflects the fears that society has about body image and the common unattainable beauty standards. Social pressures and the wide-range impact of popular culture and the media, which grows unreachable and artificial beauty standards, are the source of the girl's difficulties. The narrative depicts the girl's inner conflicts, her coping mechanisms, and the effects of social demands on her mental and emotional health. Since there was an interior monologue to explore the girl's innermost feelings and ideas, the story has a narrative framework. The girl's experiences and emotions were communicated through sensory details. Third-person limited point of view is used to tell the story, concentrating on the girl's feelings and ideas while keeping a certain amount of distance. It mostly focused on the girl's life, which allowed us to see her feelings and hardship.

      Analysis and Interpretation

      The text offers a glimpse into the mind of a young teenage girl as she experiences body issues. I personally appreciate how the author weaves realistic situations into the narrative, making it genuinely relatable and authentic. Young audiences, especially those in the midst of their teenage years during the 2000s, will likely connect with the story’s message. It addresses relevant and ongoing concerns prevalent among the youth, especially amongst young girls. Taking note that the 2000s had a more intense toxicity rate regarding body image, this story was incredible with its time. A story like this is valuable as it raises awareness about the importance of self-care and encourages the audience to prioritize their well-being rather than risking their lives to unrealistic ideals.


      With everything I adore about the story, I dislike and am disappointed by the absence of the promotion of self-love and healthy change. Although the narrative effectively illustrates the negative impacts of striving to meet unrealistic beauty expectations, it fails to emphasize the importance of self-acceptance and self-empowerment. It would have hit the audience deeper if Ana had ended up learning to embrace her true identity and if she wanted to pursue her desire of losing weight, she would have done it the healthy and sustainable way instead. This shows heavy importance to me since I, myself, have seen young girls struggle with body image and pick on what they perceive as flaws to themselves. It could have served as a powerful guidance to teach the young audience to avoid being too harsh on themselves, and to create healthy choices, especially when it comes to their appearances.

      Pacis shared her own story with body image, which relates to many of her readers. Growing up in a society that often prioritizes thinness can lead to feelings of insecurity and self-doubt. Pacis's own experiences as a teacher and her interactions with young people likely influenced her portrayal of Ana's struggles. With her personal experience, she brings a variety of viewpoints to the challenges of those who do not meet 'beauty' standards. She also highlights the emotional challenges that come with body image and other related issues, including 'peer pressure' and 'pressure from family members’.

      The literary text reflects the complex relationship with societal norms, body image perceptions, and cultural values that shape individual experiences, particularly regarding weight and identity. In many cultures, especially Filipino society, body image is heavily influenced by historical and modern ideals surrounding beauty, which often favor thinness. This creates a duality where more considerable bodies are often opposed to and discriminated against, as seen in many studies discussing the cultural beliefs that influence a teenager’s self-esteem and social acceptance. The story may also highlight issues of acceptance versus resistance to societal influence, demonstrating how characters live through their identities in a culture that often says worth is the same with physical appearance. Such dynamics must be recognized as part of knowing how body images could have deep meanings in works of literature since it is shared as live experiences like in those societies.

      The story describes the struggles of a teenage girl particularly regarding body image. It highlights how early the unrealistic beauty standards are embedded in the minds of young teenage girls. The use of a first-person narrative allows readers to understand what goes inside the mind of the protagonist, Ana. Further strengthening the connection between the reader and the protagonist. The story uses uncomplicated and natural vocabulary, which allows the text to be easily understood by the young audience. Phrases and sentences like “...hello? Totally embarrassing.”, and “That would make her a loser with a capital L.” allows the text to appeal to the audience. It mimics the tone and language teenage girls use, fostering familiarity and relatability, making it seem like the story is being told by a close friend. Having descriptions like “model-thin” show emphasis on the trivial beauty standards being upheld by magazines and overall media, causing pressure to young girls to look a certain way, that there is only one way to look right. These elements are what emphasizes the focus of the story, that the target audience can consume the point being made by the author.

      Conclusion

      Overall, Pacis’ literature gives light to the struggles individuals face with body image, societal expectations, and self-worth. With its focus on Ana, a teenage girl who experiences discrimination and slow decrease in emotional health due to her weight, and the pressure to meet society's standards. However, the story misses the importance of personal identity appreciation, leaving some readers with mixed emotions due to its lack of solutions. Nonetheless, this literary piece presents the harsh reality we face in today's modern world, where the media pushes beauty standards which leave a negative toll on individuals. Making them push the idea of being comfortable in their own skin aside. With this interpretation, let us teach younger generations, or people in general, to love themselves and ignore the media's judgment. Embracing diversity over forcing people to 'fit in' a mold set by societal standards, and build a world where generations feel accepted and valued, no matter their size and appearance.

      UCSP

      The story focuses on body shaming, especially the pressure to look a certain way to fit into society's idea of beauty. According to the World Health Organization (2019), body image concerns are a leading factor in mental health challenges among adolescents. Furthermore, a study published in Body Image: An international Journal of Research (2015) explains how the beauty standards set by society and the media make many people feel unhappy with their bodies and reinforce harmful stereotypes. These beauty ideals often promote the idea that only certain traits, like being very thin, having a specific body shape, or having perfect skin are considered attractive or desirable. The problem is, these standards are often unrealistic and impossible for most people to achieve, which can leave them feeling inadequate or like they don't measure up. In addition, according to The National Eating Disorders Association (2020) they reported that body shaming significantly contributes to the development of eating disorders, demonstrating its harmful impact on physical and mental health. With these sources, it gave us a clear picture of body shaming as a pervasive issue that demands urgent attention due to its far reaching impact on individuals and society.


      In the given literary text, it showcases the struggles faced by people above average weight. The discrimination is highlighted in the text by the treatment given by the main character’s family and friends. The protagonist faces tension at home, where well-meaning parents and siblings may unintentionally reinforce harsh beauty standards, making her feel inadequate. According to Hogenboom (2022) Crucially, this bias was influenced by others: for example, their own mothers' attitudes and beliefs about body shapes affected the outcome. Yet, the story also shows the power of love and support within families, illustrating how acceptance can help someone embrace their true self. On a larger scale, it critiques how society often stigmatizes fatness and promotes unrealistic ideals through media and cultural norms, which can lead to feelings of isolation and shame. The relationship between body image and social influences is evidenced every time the physical appearance evolves because of various factors such as the media, social media, verbal messages and social relationships, among others (Cash, 1990). Social normalities create impossible standards that fosters a negative environment encouraging harmful solutions to reach the given standard. But by centering a fat character who navigates these pressures, the story encourages readers to rethink their views on beauty and body image, reminding us all of the importance of kindness, acceptance, and celebrating our unique selves. By rejecting “diet culture” and embracing all body types, we also can shift the focus from weight loss to holistic well-being. This means nourishing our bodies with nutritious food, engaging in physical activities that we genuinely enjoy, and prioritizing self-care. When our focus is on feeling good rather than conforming to societal beauty standards, we can improve our physical health while also nurturing our mental well-being (Lpc, 2024).

      Body shaming and negative body image greatly influence mental and physical well-being. A study carried out among the students of university showed that psychological distress, low self-esteem, and eating disorders had been brought about by body shaming; indeed, over half of the population showed dissatisfaction with their bodies (Zulkifli et al., 2023). Negative body talk that is highly marked by self-criticism in self-commentary has a strong relationship to self-objectification, body shame, and disordered eating behavior among genders (Warnick et al., 2022). Moreover, social pressure to look like "the ideal" contributes to body dissatisfaction. A total of 37% of young people report being upset about their appearance, and 31% report feeling shame (Mental Health Foundation, n.d.).

      “She’s not fat. She’s what people call pleasantly plump.” This sentence from the first page of Pacis’ literary piece, "There Was This Really Fat Girl", may sound kind, but it is actually an example of indirect body shaming. Despite the soft language, it reflects a societal tendency to categorize people based on their appearance, perpetuating harmful stereotypes. According to the Girls' Attitudes Survey conducted in 2016, 94 percent of teenage girls have reported being body shamed and 57 percent of 12-20 year old’s think that the cause of their bullying was due to their appearance. These percentages are a red alert to society, as these can affect not only teenage girls but also to individuals of all genders and ages. Research indicates that individuals targeted for their appearance are more exposed to developing low self-esteem and negative body image, which can lead to mental health challenges such as depression and anxiety (The Emily Program, 2021). Moreover, according to UNESCO (2023), bullying in educational settings, which is evident in the story, can contribute to a hindrance in learning. Tackling body shaming and bullying early is a need in our modern world today, not only to improve mental health but also to create an inclusive, supportive, and safe community. By strengthening existing laws, such as the “Anti-Bullying Law” or Republic Act No. 10627 and “Safe Spaces Act” or Republic Act No. 11313, can help make individuals more confident in their own skin and safe within their communities.

      In the case of someone preventing body shaming effectively, the practice must be holistic with encouragement toward positive thinking and the acceptance of self. Based on embarkbh.com, there must be developed open dialogue concerning how he perceives body shape, form, and look compared to character since it might grow his level of self-esteem and develop strength towards resisting negative speech. Another from embarkbh.com, you should encourage them to distinguish between realistic body images and unrealistic portrayals that are usually seen in the media. In turn, these promote health and fitness over shallow beauty standards so that the attitudes of families and communities are shaped. Lastly, according to helpguide.org, a supportive environment is created, for example by surrounding oneself with positive influences as well as being able to establish boundaries against comments that may call for body shaming, creating a culture that respects and welcomes.
