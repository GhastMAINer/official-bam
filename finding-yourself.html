      "Finding Yourself"


      Introduction

      Contestant #4 is a film directed and written by Jared Joven and Kaj Palanca. Contestant #4 is a film all about exploring and becoming comfortable with one’s own identity. It explores and dives into themes relating to sexuality, identity, past, and companionship. Delving into the inner conflict of people when it comes to discovering and accepting one’s own self, despite deviating from societal norms on what is expected from them. And on how it’s easier to become comfortable and accepting of one’s identity when around and interacting with other similar individuals with the same traits and/or aspects the one represses within themselves or are not so sure on how to deal with such a revelation in their identity.


      Summary

      To summarize the film, the story begins when the film of the unknown contestant participating in the beauty pageant contests, where a boy questions the old man if he’s involved in that event. As the story progresses, the boy begins to discover more about the old man with narrative questions until the boy requests errands to sweep the house and then uncovers something in the old man’s bedroom, an old picture that has something to do with the old man’s past. He began to question his relationship status until he confessed to the old man that he’s gay. Furthermore, the old man begins to feel comfortable and discusses the topic: change. Then, the film previously from the introduction revealed that the old man was contestant #4, and then the old man sent his gratitude to the boy and promised him he’d visit him sometime. At the end, the old man goes back to his ordinary life and begins doing typical tasks like peeling apples.


      Analysis and Interpretation

      The directors and writers of Contestant #4, Jared Joven and Kaj Palanca, have little background and biography publicly available. But from the future films that director and writer Jared Joven has contributed in making, like “New Queer Visions” and “How to Die Young in Manilla”, it’s clear that there’s an undeniable gravitation towards the LGBTQ+ and more specifically the gay audience of the community. Jared Joven, possibly being gay himself, may have experienced discrimination and abuse to himself or others solely due to their sexual orientation. This could be what inspired the central theme and idea of the film Contestant #4 all about a young boy coming out of the closet and sharing that he’s gay. And the fact that Jared Joven was only 17 when he made this film, quite possibly also contextualizing this short film as a way for him as a way to express and project his own thoughts and feelings to the young boy. Maybe this is what Jared Joven was really feeling when he was having difficulties with being gay, assuming that he is, and that is what we see with the young boy in the film, as an avatar and vessel to finally express the struggle within oneself towards a broad audience.

      Getting into the more technical and literary aspects of the film, the main central idea or theme of this film is finding one’s self sexuality, one’s self identity. Basing the film off of this one idea can help deepen one’s own understanding of the film from just a story about sexuality to a story about identity and discovering who we really are. Elevating the meaning of the film from just about sexuality to the real struggle between us, ourselves, and society when figuring ourselves out. This can be further emphasized with the characters and conflict, with the old man and young boy both being gay but hesitate to share it with one another. ”Or maybe the right one didd come and you just don’t want to tell me now. Or maybe you’re just afraid to tell the truth.” Maybe it’s because of a deep seated fear of being shunned and pushed away from being different? Not being accepted? Afraid of what others will think and do from deviating from societal norms? Being judged and becoming and outcast? We can find ourselves asking these same questions when we were still exploring our own identities, if we either want to fit in or be ourselves? The setting further makes this relatable to the average Joe, as it takes place in nothing more but a simple house, an environment we are all familiar with. The setting makes the plot of the film grounded and contained, and makes the conflict simple and easy to understand as they are in a self-contained environment. Despite the story only taking place in one setting, it still allows for the character’s worldviews to change and develop. The old man, being closed off and silent about his past and thoughts at the beginning of the film as he may thing no one would accept this part and side of him, becomes more open and expresses himself freely when the young boy revealed that he’s gay. Likewise with the young boy, at first shy and reserved at fully expressing himself with the same fear of being judged and mocked, finally has the courage to because of the presence and encouragement from the old man. And this all began because the young boy found and wanted to discover more about the old man’s identity in the prospect that that may be able to help him find his. “Was that you? Hey, was that you in the clip? What were you doing?” says the young boy to the old man in the middle of watching an old film of himself.

      The influence of different cultures and backgrounds on communication, thinking, beliefs, and body language cannot be underestimated. Additionally, growing up in different time periods can have a big impact on communication methods. In the short film "Contestant #4," the two main protagonists have a significant age difference, which distinguishes them while also revealing their shared identity. Despite their differences, both men identify as gay, with the younger coming out first and the older first denying it. However, as time passes, the elder guy begins to discover commonalities in their human lives. The film depicts many disputes caused by their communication styles and speaking patterns, which can lead to accidental offense. The younger guy frequently appears insensitive in his directness and is always speaking his thoughts, whereas the older man takes a more pedagogical and nurturing approach, hoping to assist the younger man toward greater wisdom and self-improvement. Asking questions and getting the proper answers will not only relieve our brains of their curiosity, but will also help us better understand one another. It might be challenging to deal with different viewpoints and perspectives, but having self-esteem, respect for others, and being open-minded can certainly help. Where the world and society may not always align with us, and that’s okay. The world and humanity is ever changing, it’s dynamic. New values and perspectives are instilled and some slowly become more acceptable and some obsolete. It’s our duty to shape and mold those values, beliefs, and perspectives for the future generations and put a stop to this barrier which stops us from transcending as humanity, like the social issue the film is based around on.



      Conclusion and UCSP

      The film “Contenstant #4” reflects on the idea of discovering one’s own self identity amidst societal norms and expectations. The literary piece heavily emphasizes on the concept of self discovery and exploring one’s identity with somebody else. Providing a sense of commonality and comfort that one may not be able to experience normally in the society. Bringing a sense of being seen and individuality while still being accepted by others. The film calls for us to be open in our worldviews, to be more accepting and willing of things being different than usual. As these stigmas cause uncertainty and individuals locking themselves up within their own selves to escape from ever growing discrimination against the different, like the social issue of the film which is discrimination of the LGBTQ+ members. This affects millions of lives of people when it comes to their daily lives as they are physically and verbally assaulted just for standing out from societal norms. This affects our own individualism and self-esteem as people, and also promotes a very ethnocentric ideology within our society. As a society and culture, we must be able to adapt and change to the needs of the individual people that form our society. Shape our culture towards a path that is accepting and open to different views, beliefs, perspectives, etc…. We need, as a collective, to be inclusive of everyone, not exclusive, and that is what we need for ourselves to instill into our societies and cultures as we move forward as a community of individuals, all with our own special aspects that makes us us.
