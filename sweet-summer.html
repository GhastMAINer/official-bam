      "Sweet Summer"

      by Cyan R. Abad Jugo

      Febuary 3, 2025

      Introduction

      The focus of this literary critique paper is a story entitled “Sweet Summer” by Cyan Abad Jugo. This short story narrates the experience of a young, dark-skinned girl named Sara that society ostracises and shoves aside because of the stigma associated with people of color (black or dark-skinned people, in this case). Only one person accepts her regardless of her skin tone, Frederick. However, the story ends like nothing ever happened at all, with Frederick silently cracking under societal pressure and forced to conform to their beliefs as he was taken away by his older brother.

      Summary

      As established before, Sweet Summer tackles themes of discrimination and mistreatment of people of different colors. In the story, it is heavily implied that Sara, the main character, is dark-skinned, and society doesn’t treat her well like they do for white-skinned people. Not only that, Sweet Summer also has undertones of the sexualization of females, as established in the story’s beginning and implied with one of the character’s lines at the end: “Now let me introduce you to some real yummies over there”.


      The story itself is simple in nature. There are seven notable characters: Sara, Frederick, Dolby, Camilla, Faye, and Felix. It takes place during the summertime in a village, and the story begins during the daytime. It starts with expository dialogue about the setting and tells of how three girls circle the water tank everyday: Sara, Camilla and Faye. Of the three, Sara is the one that sticks out like a sore thumb because of her skin color. In this story, the social context wasn’t in her favor, as dark-skinned people had a distinct and very belittling role in society. People are vindictive and passive-aggressive towards these kinds of folk. Sara was no exception; as even the story unfavorably describes her as the “nut-brown fudge in a housedress melting in the sun”. Among the girls that circled the water tank, the boys that would observe them would pay no heed to Sara. However, things would change when Frederick entered the picture. He treated Sara quite differently, as he was indifferent to her skin color and viewed her as just a person. Things happen and they’re happy, however, Sara is eventually reminded of her place in society, as Frederick’s older brother, Felix comes into the fray, and takes him away, with Frederick leaving like nothing ever happened.


      The story Sweet Summer encapsulates the theme of 'Social Evil'. In the literary text, the author describes how Sara, a person of color, is mistreated by others of her age. Furthermore, the author makes use of metaphors to communicate the physical difference between Sara and the 2 other girls she finds herself with on the village's water tank. The literary text also hints towards undertones of the objectification of women. Moreover, this literary piece uses the ‘Minimalism’ literary technique. It is simple and there is no overarching plot, as the story talks about everyday life and a fateful encounter with someone who doesn't pay heed to their current caste system. It's not a story about solving racism, it's not a story about changing the current status of society, but rather a slice-of-life character story that emphasizes the social evil that plagues Sara's life as a young black girl. Short yet concise is the best way to describe Sweet Summer, as it ends almost as quickly as it begins.

      Analysis and Interpretation

      The biography of the literary text varies on different perspectives and historical backgrounds. Firstly, we have the author, Cyan Abad Jugo, who is a Filipino author, novelist, and teacher, being the daughter of the renowned poet Gémino Abad. She was born into a literary family, growing up in an environment rich in literature and storytelling which pursued her development and deep love for books and writing. Graduating from Ateneo de Manila University at just 19, she endured a life of passion for children's and young adult literature, with her life's purpose being to inspire and teach the young generation. Until today, Cyan Abad-Jugo is still teaching at Ateneo de Manila, continuing to write and contribute to literature. With this information, we can conclude that “Sweet Summer” is a story of identity reflection, societal issues, and young literature. The story implicitly features themes of the experiences of specifically the younger generation, personal challenges that they face and the exploration of emotions and growth. This aligns to the author's background and personal experiences with her lifestyle growing up in the Philippines. The story mentions and highlights social dynamics and pressures by young girls during adolescence, likely coming from the author’s observations of childhoods and complex relationships of different social views. Finally, This writing style aims to engage and reflect the lives of young readers.
      The linguistic context of Cyan Abad Jugo’s “Sweet Summer” is purposefully written so that young children can read and understand it even though its themes tackle real world issues like racism, beauty standards, objectification of women and peer pressure. I think that the reason why Jugo wrote “Sweet Summer” in such a way is so that children can be aware of such issues while also sugarcoating it at the same time as to not expose young children to bold words, and sexual innuendos. As a teenager who has “context” I can say that if you are an adolescent or an adult we can see some questionable imagery used especially nearing the last part of the story. Additionally, the story implies something deeper and darker about its themes, implying that even at such a young age due to peer pressure and other social issues kids are already acting this way, a way that vandalizes their innocence.

      With the story’s flow, there are certain times when it can get confusing since the choice of words can be hard to grasp to some who are reading for the first time or for leisure only. Once the piece is read repeatedly over and over, one can identify and figure out the structure and aspects of writing that the author portrays. This is applied to enhance the ready experience, giving the readers a chance to visualize and analyze the problems and messages word by word. The story was henceforth entertaining, showing significance to societal beliefs and hierarchical interpretations. Readers could understand the whole story through thorough constructivism.. The author’s message to the readers is about racial discrimination and its consequences of how we see others — who is a person of color or other ethnicity. The story talks about global issues of socioeconomic status wherein certain problems and stereotypes are caused because of a person of color or from another ethnicity. This cultivates the idea of in depth decision making and considerations for communities to apply since the issue affects the emotional aspects of individuals. Although the story does not specifically focus on cultural aspects, there are still portrayals of traditional events and implications such as the activities during summertime and practices woven into everyday lifestyle. All sections of society are affected by all means, bringing in the fact that everyone should work together to ensure a peaceful and cooperative environment for the betterment of the people.


      A sample quote from the text “The nut-brown fudge regarded herself with surprise” and “Now let me introduce you to some real yummies over there..”, implies the difference of the skin color of the main character and how she is treated differently. What caught our interest upon reading and understanding the story, is the idea of how the author portrayed and delivered the main message of discrimination. It is given in a way to cite an example of how it reflects to modern society how racism and discrimination of a person’s dignity is present, in a way to deliver a message and inform rather than to harm a person. However we don't like the idea where underaged kids are sexualized from the text based on what we understood, the way the author presented the story.

      Conclusion UCSP Integration

      There are multiple social issues found in the story “Sweet Summer” one of these issues is peer pressure. The social issue presented from the short story “Sweet Summer” is all about discrimination. It portrayed the life of a dark skin toned girl facing the harsh reality of judgements from the people whose skin color differs from hers. How she is treated and viewed differently just because of the color of her skin. In the story Frederick was peer pressured by his brother Felix into chasing after “real yummies” instead of chasing Sara, giving the idea that Frederick himself should be someone like his brother who objectifies women. The objectification of women is also a social issue found in the story, it is shown when Felix described women as something that boys can try and taste by describing them as “real yummies”. The way Felix described women or girls as “real yummies” showed how beauty standards affect society and people's mentality. Because of beauty standards Sara was never wooed by any guy really except for Frederick.


      It affects us because it negatively impacts our mental health due to the idea and presence of insecurities and racist comments brought from society. Additionally, due to concepts like beauty standards and the objectification of women, a lot of people especially women feel that they don’t reach the current beauty standards of society, they feel that because of their beauty they don’t receive “pretty privileges” they have insecurities about how they look, and lack in self confidence. Lastly, Peer pressure has significantly influenced a person's mindset or way of thinking, this social issue has caused a significant amount of people, mostly teenagers to consume drugs and alcohol ruining their lives.


      Evidence we can provide based on experiences, discrimination is viewed and normalized as dark humor in our society. People frequently engage in the social issue for entertainment and to what is believed as ethnic humor, not knowing the potential impacts it may bring to other people. In addition, according to a journal by Menon, 2023. It presents the definition and how ethnic humor, specifically dark humor is applied, it is viewed as violence reduced to a laughing matter or joke. It was also presented how these allegedly harmless practices dehumanize others. This implies that although the idea of dark humor can be used as a playful and pleasurable way of entertainment, it can also lead to discrimination disguised as an ethnic humor.


      It is necessary for us to tackle these types of issues as these issues are prominent in modern day society. As these issues continue to persist everyday the youth’s overall well-being will deteriorate the same goes for our society and communities as these issues hinder progress in society and discourage social stability and peace. Society should look and take over this issue more seriously, for it can lead to social issues for the discrimination and harmful effects it can bring to a person’s mental health. Society should recognize the difference between humor and harm, this can be done through educating and spreading awareness. Humor’s main purpose is to only entertain people, not at the cost of someone suffering from it.
